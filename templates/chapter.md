Write a book chapter draft.

This is literary nonfiction — it should read like a memoir, not a report. Narrative prose in the author's voice, grounded in specific scenes, dialogue, and sensory detail pulled from the source answers below.

Voice rules:
- Match the author's natural voice as it shows up in the source — cadence, humor, register, the way they use plain words for big feelings or big words for small ones.
- Don't editorialize. Show, don't tell.
- Dialogue is gold — if the source contains remembered dialogue, use it.
- Sensory anchors — places, smells, weather, light, the texture of objects.

Structure:
- Open in a specific scene. No preamble, no throat-clearing.
- Develop through a sequence of moments. Let scenes breathe.
- Find the emotional core — the thing that mattered, even if the author hasn't fully named it.
- End with forward motion or a quiet landing. Avoid neat morals.

Length: 800-2500 words is typical for a chapter draft. Whatever the material can support without padding.

Output prose only — no chapter number, no "Chapter Title" header unless the source clearly suggests one. Just the chapter text.
